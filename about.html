About Me

I often go by Farhan (pronounced FAAR-HAAN).

I am a postdoctoral fellow at Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI), located in Baltimore, MD.

I study how galaxies evolve, and especially how this evolution is influenced by their larger surroundings. I am well-versed in both observational and theoretical approaches to astrophysics.

I received my PhD in astronomy from New Mexico State University in summer 2024, under the supervision of Prof. Joe Burchett.

I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in physics from Reed College (Portland, OR) in 2018.

I was born and raised in Bangladesh, where I lived until 2014.
